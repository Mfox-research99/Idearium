# Idearium

**A living cross-tradition index of the Great Ideas — Western canon, Islamic, Chinese, South Asian, African, and Indigenous thought. Every author with something distinct to say has a say.**

---

## What this is

Mortimer Adler's *Syntopicon* (1952, Encyclopedia Britannica / Great Books of the Western World) was a landmark achievement: a topical index mapping 102 Great Ideas across the Western canon. It was frozen the day it went to press — constrained by physical pages, print costs, and the boundaries of one editorial team's canonical choices.

The Idearium is something larger:

- **Scope**: Western canon + non-Western traditions (Islamic, Chinese, South Asian, African, Indigenous Americas) + modern critique (20th-century political theory, depth psychology, postcolonial thought, feminist and queer theory) + living additions as scholarship grows
- **Constraint**: Relevance only — no passage quotas, no artificial limits. Every author with something genuinely distinct to say about an Idea has a say.
- **Nature**: Living — maintained and extended by AI agents, not fixed at a publication date
- **Attribution**: Every passage carries its author. The index is a record of attributed views, not a curated highlight reel.

Adler's Syntopicon asked: *what did the Great Books say about the Great Ideas?*

The Idearium asks: *what has human thought — across all traditions and all time — said about these Ideas, and what does it say now?*

## Structure

```
Ideas/          — One file per Great Idea (145+ and growing)
Terms/          — Key terms and concepts cross-referenced to Ideas
Copyrighted_Passages/  — In-copyright works: fair-use quotations, fully attributed
Great_Books_Master_Index.md  — Master source index with ingestion status
Syntopicon_Format_Spec.md    — Format specification for Idea pages
```

---

## How to use it

Each Idea page follows a consistent format:
- **Overview** — what the Idea is and why it matters
- **Western Canon** — key passages and thinkers from the Western tradition
- **Non-Western Traditions** — Islamic, Chinese/East Asian, South Asian, African, Indigenous voices
- **Modern and Critical Voices** — 20th-century political theory, depth psychology, postcolonial and feminist critique
- **Cross-references** — related Ideas and Terms

The `Copyrighted_Passages/` folder contains short fair-use quotations (typically 100–500 words) from in-copyright works, each fully attributed with author, work, date, and page reference where available. These are included under fair use for educational and research purposes.

## Scope of traditions represented

| Tradition | Coverage |
|---|---|
| Western (ancient–19th c.) | Plato, Aristotle, Augustine, Aquinas, Dante, Montaigne, Bacon, Descartes, Hobbes, Locke, Hume, Kant, Hegel, Mill, Nietzsche, and many more |
| Islamic | Al-Ghazali, Ibn Rushd, Ibn Sina, Rumi, Hafiz, Ibn Khaldun, Al-Farabi |
| Chinese / East Asian | Confucius, Mencius, Laozi, Zhuangzi, Han Feizi, Wang Yangming |
| South Asian | Upanishads, Bhagavad Gita, Kautilya, Vatsyayana, Tagore, Gandhi, Ambedkar |
| African | Ubuntu philosophy, Ama Ata Aidoo, Chinua Achebe, Wole Soyinka |
| Indigenous Americas | Haudenosaunee Great Law, Lakota oral tradition, Andean cosmology |
| Modern Western | Arendt, Rawls, Foucault, Fanon, Baldwin, Lorde, Freud, Jung, Reich, Kimmerer |
