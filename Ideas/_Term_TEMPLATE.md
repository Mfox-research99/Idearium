# Term: <Name>

## At a glance

One paragraph. State the term, its tradition, why it warrants its own page rather than folding into an Idea, and the headline polysemy. The reader should know in 30 seconds whether this term is what they came for.

> *Example for Dharma:* "Dharma (Sanskrit, धर्म, from √dhṛ 'to hold/sustain') is the central operative concept of classical Hindu thought and a load-bearing term in Buddhism and Jainism with shifted meanings. Across the corpus it carries at least four distinct senses — cosmic order, individual duty, religion-or-teaching, and truth — that are not interchangeable. The Western Ideas of Justice, Duty, Religion, and Truth each catch part of it; none catches the whole."

## Common misuses

If applicable. Modern colloquial drift, mistranslation conventions, popular reductions. Name them honestly so a reader doesn't import the misuse into the Syntopicon by accident.

> *Example for Karma:* "In modern US colloquial usage, 'karma' is often used as a folk-justice concept ('karma will get him') referring to a near-immediate cosmic comeuppance. This is not the classical sense, which operates over many lifetimes and concerns the metaphysics of action's residue, not interpersonal payback."
