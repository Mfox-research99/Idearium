# Scope and Limitations

## What this is

A curated, cross-cultural philosophical encyclopedia indexed to the Great Ideas — an expansion of Mortimer Adler's *Syntopicon* (1952) to include non-Western traditions, modern critique, and voices Adler's editorial team could not include.

## What's in it

137 Idea pages and 39 Term pages drawing on primary-source passages curated from public-domain translations (pre-1929 publication or pre-1929 translation date).

Traditions represented: Western canon (ancient through 19th century), Islamic philosophy and Sufism, Hindu and Buddhist thought, Confucian and Daoist philosophy, Persian literary tradition, Hebrew scripture, and ethnographic sources on Indigenous and shamanic traditions.

## What's missing — and why

**20th-century critical theory:** Feminist theory, postcolonial thought, Marxist critique, psychoanalytic theory beyond Freud's public-domain works — all fall behind the copyright wall. Fair-use passages from Arendt, Fanon, Baldwin, Rawls, Reich, and others are in `Copyrighted_Passages/` but not yet fully integrated into Idea pages.

**African philosophy:** Largely absent due to corpus limitations. Sub-Saharan philosophical traditions are underrepresented in pre-1929 English translation.

**Japanese, Korean, and Southeast Asian thought:** Not yet represented. A known gap for future expansion.

**Indigenous Americas depth:** The Shamanism and Ritual pages draw on ethnographic sources (Czaplicka, Bogoras). Native philosophical voices in their own words are largely absent — a corpus and translation gap.

**Stoic tradition:** Scattered. Seneca, Epictetus, and Marcus Aurelius are underrepresented relative to their importance.

**Dante's Purgatorio and Paradiso:** Only the Inferno is192 in corpus. The doctrinal center of Dante's love theology (Purgatorio XVII-XVIII) and the culminating vision (Paradiso XXXIII) are absent.

## How to use it

Each Idea page follows a consistent format: working definition, canonical passages, non-Western reframings, cross-references. Pages in draft status are marked as such and include notes on what is missing.

The `Terms/` directory contains 39 cross-cultural concept pages (Dharma, Tao, Sunyata, Wu Wei, Karuna, etc.) that provide deeper context for the reframing sections in Idea pages.

## How to contribute

New passages must be: primary-source (or fair-use secondary), fully attributed (author, work, date, translator), and genuinely distinct — not redundant with what is already on the page. Relevance is the only filter; there are no passage quotas.
